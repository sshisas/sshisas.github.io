About Me

I am a programmer specializing in Python and Java. I have designed and engineered data science software and machine learning algorithms. I created software which translated data from the membrane inlet mass spectrometer on the Scripps Pier as LabVIEW data into Python plot.ly graphs which were hosted in iframes using Github Pages on ecoobs.ucsd.edu. I also have been sponsored to attend BlackHat, and I attended a reverse engineering training program at Google San Francisco called Blackhoodie. When it comes to reverse engineering, I have reversed Minesweeper, and done many bombs and crackmes. I have also created many websites over the years, including the aforementioned ecoobs.ucsd.edu. Before attending university for computer science, I worked in cancer and infectious disease research.
